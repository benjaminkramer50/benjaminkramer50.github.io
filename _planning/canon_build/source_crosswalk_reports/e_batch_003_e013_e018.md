## Known False Positives And Source-Class Risks

- Cambridge histories are chapter-level scholarly histories. They support context, taxonomy, and corroboration, not direct inclusion by themselves.
- Translation or edition series such as Murty, Clay Sanskrit, African Writers Series, and LTI Korea primarily support availability, identity, reception, and access unless independently corroborated by anthology or curriculum rows.
- Poetry anthologies generate poem-level evidence. They do not automatically support a full poet, full collection, or complete-book row.
- Digital text collections and metadata catalogs are valuable for aliases and provenance, but they do not close complete-work source debt alone.
