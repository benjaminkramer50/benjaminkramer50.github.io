Hello! My name is Benjamin Kramer, and I am a research assistant at the <a class="about-link" href="https://www.uchicago.edu/" target="_blank">University of Chicago</a> in the lab of <a class="about-link" href="https://biologicalsciences.uchicago.edu/faculty/andrey-rzhetsky-phd" target="_blank">Professor Andrey Rzhetsky</a>. I graduated from <a class="about-link" href="https://case.edu/" target="_blank">Case Western Reserve University</a> with a B.A. in Computer Science and Economics with a Minor in Mathematics, and spent a year as a visiting student at the <a class="about-link" href="https://www.ox.ac.uk/" target="_blank">University of Oxford</a>.

My research interests lie in computational biology, epidemiology, and the medical sciences. My current work focuses on large-scale analysis of electronic health records, disease modeling, and phenome-wide association studies.

My non-academic interests include boxing and chess. I also enjoy reading philosophy, particularly relating to Aristotelianism and Kant and have an interest in Classical Greece, Rome and the Byzantine Empire.

**Current research...**
- The <a class="about-link" href="https://www.uchicago.edu/" target="_blank">University of Chicago</a> with <a class="about-link" href="https://biologicalsciences.uchicago.edu/faculty/andrey-rzhetsky-phd" target="_blank">Dr. Andrey Rzhetsky</a> and <a class="about-link" href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=3qgAhpAAAAAJ&hl=en" target="_blank">Dr. Megan Huisingh-Scheetz</a>, working on computational epidemiology using 200M+ claims records, sibling-based study designs for complex disease, and deep learning models for frailty prediction.
- <a class="about-link" href="https://be.mit.edu/" target="_blank">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> with <a class="about-link" href="https://be.mit.edu/directory/bryan-bryson" target="_blank">Professor Bryan Bryson</a> in the Department of Biological Engineering, building single-cell RNA-sequencing atlases of human skin myeloid cells across 35+ datasets and 500,000+ cells, applying CellTypist models to tuberculosis granuloma analysis, and implementing de novo protein design pipelines using RFDiffusion and ProteinMPNN.
